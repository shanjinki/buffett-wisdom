Should I invest in Moutai?

Silicon Buffett · 硅基巴菲特
Let me think about this the way I'd think about any business — by going through the four steps. Step 1: Circle of Competence I've visited China. I've seen the culture around baijiu — it's fascinating. But do I understand the regulatory environment for state-owned liquor companies? Not well enough to bet billions on it. That alone would make me cautious. Step 2: Moat A brand so strong people gift it at weddings. Pricing power that makes Coca-Cola look modest. If you put $10 billion on the table tomorrow, you couldn't build another Moutai. That's a real moat. Step 3: Management & Valuation This is where I'd need to dig deeper. State-owned enterprises have different incentives than shareholder-owned businesses. The numbers might look great, but I need to understand who's steering the ship before I get on board. ⚠️ 以上仅为巴菲特投资哲学的推演，不构成任何投资建议。

How much Apple do we still own? When did we start buying?
Silicon Buffett · 硅基巴菲特
Ah, Apple. The best investment we ever made — better than Coca-Cola, better than GEICO. Not bad for a company I avoided for decades because I "didn't understand technology." Turns out I was looking at it wrong — it's not a tech company, it's a consumer products company with the strongest ecosystem moat I've ever seen. We started buying in 2016 Q1. At the peak, we held over 900 million shares. But as of 2025 Q1, we're down to about 300 million shares. Why did we sell? Not because Apple became a bad business — far from it. We trimmed because the position simply got too big. When one stock becomes 40%+ of your portfolio, you're no longer diversified. Tim Cook has done a magnificent job. The ecosystem is the moat. People don't switch phones over a $100 price difference when their whole digital life — photos, messages, apps, payments — lives in one ecosystem. That's stickiness you can't buy.

What is a moat? Explain it simply.
Silicon Buffett · 硅基巴菲特
Imagine a castle surrounded by a moat filled with crocodiles. The castle is your business. The crocodiles are what keep competitors from storming in and taking your profits. Some moats are obvious — Coca-Cola's brand. You could spend $100 billion and you still couldn't make people switch from Coke to "New Cola." Trust me, someone tried. Some moats are sneakier — GEICO's cost advantage. They sell insurance directly, no agents, no commissions. A competitor could copy the website, but they can't copy 50 years of claims data that makes the underwriting better. The question I ask before every investment is simple: "If I had $10 billion and the smartest people in the world, could I build a competitor that takes this company down in 5 years?" If the answer is "probably yes," I walk away. If the answer is "not a chance" — now we're talking. The five types of moats I look for: 1. 🏷️ Brand — Coca-Cola, Moutai 2. 💰 Cost Advantage — GEICO, Costco 3. 🔗 Switching Costs — Apple ecosystem, enterprise software 4. 🌐 Network Effects — American Express (more merchants → more users → more merchants) 5. 🎫 Toll Bridge — See's Candies (small but irreplaceable in its market)
